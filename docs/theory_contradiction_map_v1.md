# Theory Contradiction Map v1

## Purpose

This note maps the main theoretical disagreements that still matter for
`alife_biosphere`.

It does not try to resolve them permanently.
It does something simpler and more useful:

- identify where the literature is aligned
- identify where it is split
- identify what the project should treat as settled enough to build on, versus
  unsettled enough to describe cautiously

This is a theory-layer coordination document.

## 1. How To Read This Map

Each topic is split into:

- `shared ground`
- `active disagreement`
- `current working lean`
- `why the disagreement matters`

The `current working lean` is not final truth.
It is the least distorted position suggested by the current library.

## 2. Major Theoretical Contradictions

## 2.1 Founder Effects And Lineage Novelty

Shared ground:

- founder events and bottlenecks can matter
- migration into new contexts can reweight selection

Active disagreement:

- some traditions treat founder events as potentially powerful engines of rapid
  novelty
- skeptical traditions argue that bottlenecks alone are weak evidence and are
  often overclaimed

Current working lean:

- founder events matter as structured colonization events
- bottlenecks alone are not enough to justify strong lineage-innovation claims

Why it matters:

- if we ignore the disagreement, we will overinterpret any small colonizing
  subgroup as a new lineage mechanism

## 2.2 Grouping Versus Higher-Level Individuality

Shared ground:

- higher-level individuality requires more than spatial grouping
- cooperation alone is insufficient

Active disagreement:

- the literature differs on how strongly ecological scaffolding, life-cycle
  structure, and group-level statistics should count before strong
  individuality claims are made

Current working lean:

- grouping, ecological role, and group-level selection should all be treated as
  intermediate claim levels
- strong higher-level individuality remains a high bar

Why it matters:

- this is probably the easiest place in the whole project to overclaim

## 2.3 Novelty Versus Open-Endedness

Shared ground:

- novelty matters
- long-run innovation is central to open-ended systems

Active disagreement:

- the literature does not support one single criterion for open-endedness
- there is no consensus that long runtime, diversity, novelty, or complexity
  alone is enough

Current working lean:

- open-endedness should remain plural
- novelty, structural change, ecological restructuring, and cumulative lineage
  persistence should stay separate

Why it matters:

- otherwise we will mistake vivid traces or large variation for deep
  open-endedness

## 2.4 Stability Versus Resilience

Shared ground:

- stable systems and resilient systems are not the same
- recovery structure matters

Active disagreement:

- the adaptive-cycle and resilience literature is useful, but there is no fully
  agreed lightweight operationalization for small synthetic worlds

Current working lean:

- use resilience and recovery as distinct axes
- avoid assuming that smooth equilibrium is the best sign of health

Why it matters:

- if we optimize for smoothness, we may build a dead but tidy world

## 2.5 Regime Shifts Versus Ordinary Release/Reorganization

Shared ground:

- abrupt changes and major reorganizations can happen
- warning-style thinking is valuable

Active disagreement:

- not every abrupt change is a critical transition
- not every release/reorganization cycle deserves to be called a regime shift

Current working lean:

- keep `regime shift` as a stronger term than `abrupt change`
- require persistence and changed future selection, not only sharp time-series
  movement

Why it matters:

- this affects how we interpret disturbance and recovery traces

## 2.6 Honesty In Signaling

Shared ground:

- signaling can support coordination
- deception can also be stable

Active disagreement:

- classic cost-focused signaling traditions are not sufficient to explain all
  honesty
- newer work emphasizes trade-offs, repeated interaction, verification, and
  private information

Current working lean:

- honesty should not be modeled as "message cost solves it"
- repeated interaction, trust, and verification deserve more weight

Why it matters:

- naive signaling implementations will likely produce the wrong theory of
  honesty

## 2.7 Archive Size And Cultural Benefit

Shared ground:

- social learning and cumulative culture can be powerful
- cultural transmission can create real adaptive gains

Active disagreement:

- more available models or more archive content is not automatically better
- visibility, burden, filtering, and validation all change outcomes

Current working lean:

- bounded, validated, domain-sensitive archive access is more defensible than
  "bigger archive is better"

Why it matters:

- otherwise archive design drifts into unlimited memory romanticism

## 2.8 Modularity And Stability

Shared ground:

- modularity often matters
- connectivity structure changes system behavior

Active disagreement:

- modularity is not uniformly stabilizing
- connectedness cannot be summarized by one scalar without losing important
  differences

Current working lean:

- modularity is informative, but not inherently good
- connectedness should remain a profile of different structural summaries

Why it matters:

- otherwise we will attach value to one graph statistic that cannot carry the
  full interpretation

## 2.9 Parasites: Failure Mode Or Complexity Engine

Shared ground:

- parasites and exploiters can destabilize worlds
- antagonistic coevolution can also generate complexity

Active disagreement:

- the literature does not support a simple answer to whether parasites are net
  creative or net destructive
- outcomes depend strongly on spatial structure, migration, and defense

Current working lean:

- antagonists should remain mechanism candidates, not be treated only as noise
  or bugs

Why it matters:

- removing antagonism too early may erase one of the strongest routes to
  ecological richness

## 2.10 Symbiosis And Composite Organization

Shared ground:

- persistent beneficial association can create meaningful ecological structure

Active disagreement:

- literature differs on when such associations should count as units of
  selection or higher-level individuals
- holobiont-style language is especially easy to overextend

Current working lean:

- persistent cross-lineage integration should be allowed in theory
- but should be described first in ecological, not strong individuality, terms

Why it matters:

- it keeps the project open to non-kin organization without romantic overclaim

## 3. The Current Theory Posture

Across the whole contradiction map, one pattern repeats:

```text
the literature usually supports the direction of the project,
but often disagrees about thresholds, interpretation strength, and what counts
as enough evidence for a stronger claim.
```

That means the project can move forward confidently on mechanism design while
still speaking cautiously about:

- individuality
- open-endedness
- regime shifts
- archive value
- parasite creativity

## 4. What This Map Suggests For Writing Style

When a concept sits inside an active disagreement, the project should prefer:

- descriptive language over celebratory language
- intermediate claims over maximal claims
- explicit uncertainty over implied consensus

Examples:

- say `candidate group-level selection` before `new individual`
- say `abrupt reorganization` before `regime shift` unless persistence is clear
- say `archive transfer gain under these conditions` before `culture works`

## 5. What Still Remains Beyond This Map

This document does not yet quantify disagreement strength.

It also does not turn every disagreement into a claim-evidence row.
Those remain separate layers.
